"Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet..." – If you've been on the internet for more than a day, you've likely seen this text. It's the standard placeholder text used by the design and printing industry for over 500 years. But it’s not just random letters jumbled together; it has a rich history dating back to the Roman Empire and serves a vital psychological purpose in design.

In this guide, we’ll explore the origins of Lorem Ipsum, why it’s scientifically better than repeating "Text here text here," and—most importantly—how leaving it in your production code can disastrously affect your SEO rankings in 2026.

The Ancient Origins (45 BC)

Contrary to popular belief, Lorem Ipsum is not random gibberish. It has roots in a piece of classical Latin literature from 45 BC, making it over 2,000 years old.

The Cicero Connection

It comes from sections 1.10.32 and 1.10.33 of "de Finibus Bonorum et Malorum" (The Extremes of Good and Evil) by Marcus Tullius Cicero, a famous Roman orator. This book was a treatise on the theory of ethics, very popular during the Renaissance.

The Original Text
"Neque porro quisquam est qui dolorem ipsum quia dolor sit amet, consectetur, adipisci velit..."

// Translation:
// "There is no one who loves pain itself, who seeks after it and wants to have it, simply because it is pain..."

In the 1500s, an unknown typesetter took a galley of type and scrambled it to make a type specimen book. He stripped common words to ensure it looked like "real" text but meant nothing.

Why Designers Use Placeholder Text

Why not just write "Content coming soon" or copy-paste a news article?

  1. Visual Distribution: Lorem Ipsum has a more-or-less normal distribution of letters, as opposed to using "Content here, content here," which makes the text look blocky and unrealistic.
  2. Focus on Design: If you use readable English (or the client's native language), the viewer gets distracted by reading the content instead of looking at the layout. Lorem Ipsum forces the brain to ignore the words and focus on fonts, spacing, and hierarchy.
  3. Language Neutrality: It looks international but doesn't imply a specific language or region.

Modern Alternatives & Generators

The SEO Danger of Lorem Ipsum

Warning: Check Your Production Code!

Leaving "Lorem Ipsum" in a live website is a massive SEO signal of low quality. Google bots crawl this text and categorize your page as "placeholder" or "duplicate content," often de-indexing it completely.

The Fix: Always use a linter or a pre-commit hook in your development workflow to scan for "Lorem ipsum" strings before deploying to production. This simple step can save your search rankings.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does "Lorem Ipsum" actually mean? +
It doesn't mean anything in its current form. It is a corrupted version of the Latin word "dolorem" (pain). The typesetter in the 1500s cut off the first syllable "do-", leaving "lorem". So, roughly, it's gibberish derived from a sentence about "loving pain."
Is Lorem Ipsum free to use? +
Yes, entirely. The text is based on a 2,000-year-old public domain work by Cicero. Use it in personal items, commercial projects, software, or print without any attribution or royalty fees.
How do I generate Lorem Ipsum in VS Code? +
If you have Emmet installed (built-in to VS Code), simply type lorem and hit Tab. It will expand into a 30-word paragraph. You can also type lorem10 for 10 words, or lorem*5 for 5 paragraphs. It's a massive time-saver for developers.
Can I use Lorem Ipsum for foreign languages? +
You can, but it might look odd. Lorem Ipsum looks like English or Romance languages (French, Spanish, Italian). If you are designing for Chinese, Arabic, or Russian, the character density and spacing are totally different. You should use language-specific generators (like "Chinese Lorem Ipsum") to test the layout accurately.
Why does Lorem Ipsum start with "Lorem"? +
It's a truncation error from the original source. The sentence in Cicero's book was "Neque porro quisquam est qui dolorem ipsum..." (There is no one who loves pain itself...). The page break likely cut off the "do-", creating the nonsensical word "Lorem" that has stuck for 5 centuries.
Is it bad accessibility practice? +
Yes, for screen readers. A screen reader will try to pronounce the Latin gibberish, which sounds confusing and annoying to blind users. If you must use it in a prototype, consider adding aria-hidden="true" to the container so screen readers skip it, or clearly label it as "Placeholder Text".
Are there funny versions of Lorem Ipsum? +
Hundreds! From Bacon Ipsum (meat names) to Star Wars Ipsum, Samuel L. Jackson Ipsum (explicit!), and Cheese Ipsum. They are fun for internal mocks but be careful: accidentally deploying "Samuel L. Ipsum" to a client site can get you fired due to the profanity!
